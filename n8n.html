What we do

The world as our office.

We're Caesar, Stijn and Max, three founders who build like everyone our age: heads down, screens on. This summer we do it differently. We walk 120 km of the Camino and build as we go, laptop in the backpack, Starlink overhead, glasses for a screen. The office fits in a bag, and the agents that run our build come with us. Voice in, a joystick to steer, the screen on our face, hands and eyes free for the trail.

Where n8n comes in

Set the flow, then look up.

n8n is the layer that runs our build while we move. We're not hunched over a dashboard keeping it alive: we set up the flows and they carry on, connecting the tools, running the agents, handling the work in between. The best automation doesn't ask for more of your attention. It hands it back.

What it does
n8n orchestrates the flows behind our build: the agents, the tools, the steps between them. The busywork that would otherwise keep us at a desk.
Why it fits
Automation that runs on its own is what lets us work from anywhere. Set it going, put the laptop away, keep walking. The flow doesn't need us watching it.
What it gives back
Time to look up. The point isn't how hard we can push it, but how much attention it hands back to us on the trail.

The ask

What we're asking.

We're looking for a sponsor for the experiment. What exactly that looks like, we'd rather shape together on a call. Here's the frame.

What we're looking for
A partner who helps make six days on the trail possible, and in return becomes part of a story worth watching. We already have Watermelon, Remote, Helloprint and Wispr Flow on board. We'd love n8n in the mix, because this experiment is squarely about what n8n does.
What you get
n8n featured in everything we make, from the documentary to the shorts to the agent-flow animations that come straight out of your product. The story of automation that gives your time back, lived by three founders who set the flows running and kept walking, not claimed in an ad.
